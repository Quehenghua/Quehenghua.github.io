## About Me
majoring in Cybersecurity at Sichuan University.
I am currently a junior student at **Sichuan University**, majoring in double degree in Cybersecurity and Law. My academic interests include **content security**, **computer vision**, **deep learning**, and **multimodal understanding**.

My current research focuses on **Chinese harmful meme detection**, especially the detection and identification of harmful content distributed across visual and textual modalities. Beyond multimodal content safety, I am also interested in **deepfake detection** and **computer vision**, particularly in detecting AI-generated or manipulated images, and capturing subtle visual artifacts and cross-modal inconsistencies.

## Publications

### AMKW: Adaptive Multi-view Knowledge Weighting for Chinese Harmful Meme Detection

**Henghua Que**, et al.  
Accepted by **International Conference on Intelligent Computing (ICIC 2026)**.

This work proposes **AMKW**, a framework for Chinese harmful meme detection and identification. It addresses the distributional differences of harmful cues across modalities among different memes and introduces a signal-guided adaptive weighting mechanism to associate original multimodal features with multi-view semantic interpretations generated by a multimodal large language model.

## Projects

### Chinese Harmful Meme Detection

This project focuses on detecting harmful semantics in Chinese memes by modeling visual, textual, and cross-modal cues. The work explores how multimodal representations and semantic knowledge generated by multimodal large language models can be adaptively integrated for harmful content understanding.

**Keywords:** Multimodal Learning, Harmful Meme Detection, Content Security, Vision-Language Understanding

### LLM-ABM Based Generative Policy Simulation Platform

This project develops a generative policy simulation framework based on large language models and agent-based modeling. It integrates real-world microdata, LLM-generated heterogeneous agents, and policy intervention simulation to support computational policy evaluation and intelligent governance.

**Keywords:** LLM-Agent Systems, Agent-Based Modeling, Policy Simulation, Computational Social Science

### Short Video Content Safety Governance System

This project designs a system for detecting, analyzing, and governing potentially harmful or controversial content on short-video platforms. It integrates multimodal video analysis, comment-section risk detection, and heterogeneous graph-based account behavior modeling.

**Keywords:** Content Safety, Multimodal Detection, Comment Analysis, Heterogeneous Graphs, Platform Governance
